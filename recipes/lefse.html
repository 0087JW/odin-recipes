Norwegian Lefse Treat

Lefse is a potato pancake coated in white or brown sugar and rolled into a tasty dessert. It is a family tradition and I often make it and use shaped cutters before rolling it to give the lefse a holiday flair.

Ingredients

10lbs potatoes (peeled) or equally dried potato flakes, 1/2 butter, 1/3 heavy cream, 1tbsp salt, 1 tbsp white sugar, 2 1/2 cups all purpose flour.

Steps

  • Place potatoes in a large pot and cover with water, bring to a boil. Reduce to med-low heat and simmer until soft, then drain.
  • Put potatoes through potato ricer (skip this if using potato flakes).
  • In a large bowl add potatoes, butter, cream, salt, and sugar. Let cool to room temp.
  • Sift flour and add to potato mixture to form a soft dough.
  • Use a tablespoon (or smaller) to create a ball of dough and place onto a lightly floured surface, flatten dough until 1/4 inch thick then place on griddle over high heat.
  • Cook lefse until brown and blistering (1 minute a side). Place lefse on plate to cool, as an added treat gently sprinkle cinnimon+sugar on each side.
